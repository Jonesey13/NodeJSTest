From 2011 to 2015 (2016 technically speaking) I worked at the Cambridge Centre for Analysis as a PhD research student working in the field of Compressed Sensing (related to Fourier analysis and information theory).

My supervisor during this time was Anders Hansen .

My research consisted of a theoretical and an applied component. The theoretical component assessed which wavelet bases and sampling patterns were feasible for compressed sensing of Fourier inverse problems using a notion called incoherence.

This work was separated into one and multidimensional cases. The one-dimensional case was published in IEEE Tranactions on Information Theory in 2016. The multi-dimensional paper was never submitted (as I had already left academia by the time the first paper was published) but its contents can be found in my thesis (see below)

The applied component introduced compressed sensing to the field of Helium atom scattering (a surface-sensitive relative of the field of neutron atom scattering).

This was collaborative work with Surfaces, Microstructure and Fracture (SMF) group at the cavendish site.
